## The Allure of This Sacred Site

### History and Origins

The discovery of Ryusendo Cave dates back approximately 1,200 years to the Heian period. Legend tells of a local hunter who stumbled upon this cave while pursuing game, and since then, it has been carefully protected by the local community as a "sacred place where dragons dwell."

Formal academic surveys began in the 1920s. Subsequent explorations revealed new underground lakes and stalactites, leading to the cave's designation as a National Natural Monument in 1938. The currently confirmed total length of the cave extends approximately 4,088 meters, though it's believed to continue much deeper‚Äîits full extent remains unexplored.

The name "Ryusendo" derives from the legend of a dragon deity dwelling within the cave. According to local lore, during a severe drought, villagers performed rain-invoking rituals in this cave, and blessed rains immediately began to fall. This tale is still passed down through generations today.

### Architecture and Natural Features

Ryusendo's greatest attraction is undoubtedly its three underground lakes. The first lake reaches a depth of 35 meters, the second 38 meters, and the third an astounding 98 meters. The water transparency measures 41.5 meters‚Äîamong the highest in the world‚Äîso clear that the lake bottom is visible, creating the mesmerizing blue known as "Dragon Blue."

The cave maintains a constant temperature of approximately 10-11¬∞C (50-52¬∞F) year-round, feeling refreshingly cool in summer and surprisingly warm in winter. The stalactites hanging from the ceiling, stalagmites rising from the floor, and curtain-like limestone formations are said to grow just one centimeter every 100 years‚Äînatural artworks created over hundreds of millions of years.

The area called "Gekkyuden" (Moon Palace) is particularly remarkable, where pure white stalactites spread across the ceiling like moonlight illuminating the space, capturing the hearts of countless visitors.

### What Makes This Spot Special

What sets Ryusendo apart from other limestone caves is the overwhelming presence of its underground lakes. With unparalleled transparency and depth, these lakes leave viewers speechless with their beauty. The cobalt blue water surface illuminated by lighting appears like a gateway to another world.

The cave also houses the "Ryusen New Cave Science Museum," where visitors can learn about how limestone caves form and the creatures that inhabit them. Exhibits featuring rare cave-dwelling creatures found only here, such as cave freshwater clams, are among the highlights.

Following recovery from typhoon damage in 2016, new lighting equipment was installed, enabling even more fantastical atmospheric presentations.

## Visitor's Guide

### Basic Viewing Etiquette

Ryusendo is both a sacred site and a precious Natural Monument. Please observe these guidelines:

1. **Don't Touch the Stalactites**: The oils and warmth from hands can stop stalactite growth. View only from outside the barriers.

2. **View Quietly**: Sound echoes easily in the cave, and loud voices disturb other visitors. Maintain quiet behavior appropriate for a sacred space.

3. **Take Your Trash**: To preserve the cave's pristine environment, always take your trash with you.

4. **Limit Flash Photography**: It may affect the cave ecosystem.

### Recommended Viewing Route

**Entrance ‚Üí Hyakken Corridor (approx. 5 min)**: Just after entering the cave, this passage of about 100 meters gradually draws you into the underground world.

**Chomei-no-Fuchi / Pool of Longevity (approx. 3 min)**: A clear stream flowing through the cave. Legend says drinking this water brings longevity.

**Gekkyuden / Moon Palace (approx. 5 min)**: Pure white stalactite formations spreading across the ceiling. One of the most beautiful photo spots.

**First Underground Lake (approx. 5 min)**: 35 meters deep. The first underground lake you encounter‚Äîits transparency is stunning.

**Second Underground Lake (approx. 5 min)**: 38 meters deep. The cobalt blue illuminated by lighting is fantastical.

**Third Underground Lake (approx. 10 min)**: 98 meters deep. Ryusendo's greatest highlight. A place to linger and contemplate.

**Standard Viewing Time**: Approximately 40 minutes to 1 hour; 1 hour 30 minutes for thorough viewing

### Dress Code and What to Bring

- **Clothing**: The cave is about 10¬∞C (50¬∞F), making long sleeves essential even in summer. Wear comfortable walking shoes (sneakers recommended).
- **What to Bring**: Camera, handkerchief (humidity is high), small backpack
- **What to Avoid**: High heels, sandals, miniskirts (many stairs)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I visit in a wheelchair or with a stroller?**
A: The cave has many stairs, making wheelchair or stroller access difficult. Baby carriers are possible, but be cautious as some areas are slippery.

**Q: Can I bring pets?**
A: Pets are not allowed in the cave. There are no pet-sitting facilities in the parking lot.

**Q: Can I eat or drink inside the cave?**
A: Eating and drinking inside the cave is prohibited. Please use the rest house before or after your visit.

**Q: Is it okay for people with claustrophobia?**
A: The viewing paths are relatively wide with good ceiling height, so most people can tour without problems. However, there are some narrow sections, so please consult with staff beforehand if concerned.
