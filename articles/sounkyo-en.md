Located in the heart of Hokkaido within Daisetsuzan National Park, Sounkyo Gorge is a magnificent canyon formed by a massive eruption of Mount Daisetsu approximately 30,000 years ago. Stretching about 24 kilometers along the Ishikari River, the towering cliffs rise 100 to 200 meters high, overwhelming visitors with their sheer scale.

The Ainu people called this place "Souunbetsu" (river with many waterfalls) and revered it as a sacred site from ancient times. The columnar joint cliffs that line the canyon resemble stone pillars reaching toward the heavens. The twin waterfalls known as "Ginga Falls" (Galaxy Falls) and "Ryusei Falls" (Meteor Falls) are designated among Japan's 100 Famous Waterfalls.

With the onsen town as a base, visitors can enjoy the spectacular views of Daisetsuzan via ropeway while taking in the canyon's beauty. Sounkyo Gorge is a healing and regenerative power spot where you can experience the full force of Hokkaido's natural power.

## The Allure of This Sacred Site

### History and Origins

Sounkyo Gorge's history began with a massive eruption of Mount Daisetsu approximately 30,000 years ago. As the erupted lava cooled rapidly, it formed the distinctive columnar joints. Subsequently, erosion by the Ishikari River carved out the canyon landscape we see today.

The name "Sounkyo" was given by travel writer Keigetsu Omachi when he visited in 1921 (Taisho 10). Meaning "gorge where clouds rise in layers," it describes the mystical scenery created by mist covering the canyon and the columnar joint cliffs.

The Ainu people called this place "Souunbetsu" (river with many waterfalls) and revered it as a sacred site. Particularly, Ginga Falls and Ryusei Falls appear in Ainu mythology and were objects of spiritual worship.

Hot springs were discovered in the Meiji era, and development as a tourist destination began in the Taisho period. Since being designated part of Daisetsuzan National Park in 1934 (Showa 9), it has attracted numerous tourists as one of Hokkaido's premier scenic spots.

### Architecture and Natural Features

Sounkyo Gorge's most striking feature is the columnar joint cliffs that extend for approximately 24 kilometers. As the lava cooled, regular fractures formed, creating a distinctive terrain of bundled pentagonal and hexagonal pillars. The cliffs reach heights of 100 to 200 meters, presenting an awe-inspiring spectacle.

The most famous attractions are Ginga Falls (Galaxy Falls) and Ryusei Falls (Meteor Falls). Ginga Falls has a drop of 120 meters, flowing down in thin white threads like silk, earning it the nickname "Female Falls." Meanwhile, Ryusei Falls has a 90-meter drop, cascading powerfully in one great rush, known as the "Male Falls." The view from "Sobakudai" observation platform, where both waterfalls can be seen simultaneously, is breathtaking.

From Sounkyo Onsen, a ropeway and lift system takes visitors up to the 7th station (7-gome) of Mount Kurodake (elevation 1,984 meters). From the summit, a magnificent panorama of the Daisetsu mountain range spreads out. In July, Japan's earliest autumn colors appear, and spectacular fall foliage can be enjoyed from late September.

### What Makes This Spot Special

What sets Sounkyo Gorge apart from other scenic locations is the intersection of three natural forces: "volcano," "water," and "hot springs."

The columnar joint cliffs born from volcanic activity, the waterfalls cascading down these cliffs, and the hot springs welling up from deep underground‚Äîit is extremely rare to find all of these concentrated in one place.

Sounkyo is also famous for offering "Japan's earliest autumn colors." At higher elevations on Daisetsu, fall foliage begins as early as late August, with the entire mountain turning red and gold by mid-September. As the saying goes, "Autumn in Japan begins at Daisetsuzan," drawing visitors from across the country seeking autumn colors.

In winter, the "Sounkyo Hyobaku (Ice Waterfall) Festival" is held, creating a fantastical world of frozen waterfalls and illuminated ice sculptures.

## Visitor's Guide

### How to Enjoy Sounkyo Gorge

Since Sounkyo Gorge is a natural power spot, there are no specific worship rituals. Here are recommended ways to fully experience Sounkyo's power.

**1. View Ginga Falls and Ryusei Falls**
About 5 minutes by car from Sounkyo Onsen. A short walk from the parking area leads to both waterfalls. A 20-minute trail leads to "Sobakudai" observation platform for stunning simultaneous views of both falls. Let the waterfall's negative ions wash over you.

**2. Kurodake Ropeway**
From the ropeway station (5-minute walk from Sounkyo Onsen), reach the 5th station (elevation 1,300 meters) in 7 minutes. Transfer to the lift to reach the 7th station (elevation 1,520 meters). The summit station has an observation deck with panoramic views of the Daisetsu mountain range.

**3. Gorge Walking**
Walking trails are maintained throughout Sounkyo Onsen town. Walk along the Ishikari River while viewing the columnar joint cliffs up close. Takes about 30 minutes to 1 hour.

**4. Purify in the Hot Springs**
Sounkyo Onsen features simple sulfur springs. Said to be effective for neuralgia, muscle pain, and fatigue recovery. The outdoor baths overlooking great nature are exceptional. Day-use bathing facilities are plentiful.

### Spending Time as a Power Spot

**Deep Breathing Before the Waterfalls**: Stand before Ginga Falls and Ryusei Falls, close your eyes, and breathe deeply. Feel the sound and spray of the falls with your whole body for purification.

**Take Time to Look Up at the Cliffs**: Gaze up at the columnar joint cliffs and feel the flow of tens of thousands of years. You should realize how small your worries are.

**Meditate in the Hot Springs**: Soak in the outdoor baths and take time to face yourself. Let the energy of great nature seep into your body and mind.

### Dress Code and What to Bring

- **Clothing**: Bring long sleeves even in summer as it's cool. Temperatures drop further on the ropeway. Comfortable walking shoes are essential.
- **What to Bring**: Camera, warm clothing (spring/autumn/winter), rain gear, drinks
- **Note**: This is bear country, so carry a bell or bear spray for peace of mind. Stay on marked trails only.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: When is the best time for autumn foliage?**
A: Near the Daisetsuzan summit is mid-September; around Sounkyo Onsen is early October. Timing varies by year, so check the tourism association website for the latest information.

**Q: Do I need a reservation for the ropeway?**
A: No reservation needed; same-day boarding is available. However, during autumn foliage season it gets crowded, so earlier times are recommended. May close in bad weather.

**Q: Are there bears?**
A: Daisetsuzan is brown bear habitat. When walking the trails, ring a bell and travel in groups. The area around the onsen town is relatively safe.

**Q: Can I visit in winter?**
A: Hot springs and ropeway (may close in bad weather) operate in winter. The Hyobaku Festival from late January to March is a popular winter event.
