Located in Mikuni Town, Sakai City, Fukui Prefecture, Tojinbo is a magnificent sea cliff carved by the rough waves of the Sea of Japan. The columnar joint rock walls stretching approximately 1 kilometer reach heights of about 25 meters and are designated as a National Natural Monument and Place of Scenic Beauty.

These rare pyroxene andesite columnar joints are said to exist at only three locations in the world at this scale. This spectacular landscape, formed by volcanic activity approximately 13 million years ago, is a power spot where visitors can experience nature's awesome power, attracting about 1.5 million visitors annually.

The name "Tojinbo" is said to derive from a legend about a monk who once lived in this area. This place where nature's raw power and historical legend intersect is visited by many at life's turning points, offering a power spot that resets the mind and gives strength to take new steps forward.

## The Allure of This Sacred Site

### History and Origins

A sad legend is associated with the name Tojinbo. Long ago, there was a monk named Tojinbo at Heisenji Temple (in present-day Katsuyama City). He was said to be violent and troublesome to the villagers, and was pushed off these cliffs by villagers who held a grudge against him.

It is said that the sea remained rough for 49 days afterward, leading to this place being called "Tojinbo." However, this is merely legend, and there are various theories about the actual origin of the name.

Geologically, Tojinbo was formed by volcanic activity approximately 13 million years ago. As magma cooled rapidly near the surface, regular fractures (joints) formed, creating the current columnar rock formations.

It was designated as a National Natural Monument and Place of Scenic Beauty in 1935 (Showa 10). In 1972 (Showa 47), it was incorporated into Echizen-Kaga Kaigan Quasi-National Park. Today, it is Fukui Prefecture's representative tourist attraction, drawing many visitors from Japan and abroad.

### Architecture and Natural Features

Tojinbo's greatest feature is its "pyroxene andesite columnar joints." This is an extremely rare geological formation worldwide, and similar-scale examples are said to exist only at Mount Kumgang on the Korean Peninsula and Norway's west coast.

Columnar joints are polygonal (mainly pentagonal and hexagonal) columnar fractures that form when magma contracts in volume as it cools and solidifies. At Tojinbo, these columnar rocks extend continuously for about 1 kilometer, reaching a maximum height of approximately 25 meters.

From the tip of the cliff called "Oike," a grand panorama of the Sea of Japan spreads out. On clear days, you can see as far as the Noto Peninsula, and at sunset, the sun sinking into the Sea of Japan creates a spectacular view.

From Tojinbo Tower (55 meters high), you can overlook the entire columnar joint formation and the Sea of Japan. On good weather days, it's a viewpoint from which you can see all the way to the Hakusan mountain range.

### What Makes This Spot Special

What sets Tojinbo apart from other scenic spots is its "raw natural power" and "ability to help people reflect on life."

Standing on the sea cliffs and looking down at the rough waves of the Sea of Japan, you realize human smallness before nature's overwhelming power. This experience relativizes daily worries and resets the heart.

Tojinbo is also known for being visited by many at "life's turning points." When facing major decisions or feeling stuck, standing in this place is said to broaden perspectives and reveal new paths.

The columnar joints viewed from the sea on a sightseeing boat offer a different kind of impact than viewing from land. You can enjoy this natural artwork from various angles.

## Visitor's Guide

### How to Enjoy Tojinbo

Tojinbo is a natural spot, so there are no specific worship rituals. Enjoy Tojinbo's attractions with this course:

**1. Walk the Sea Cliffs**
Walk the main promenade to view the columnar joint cliffs up close. About 10 minutes' walk from the parking lot to the tip "Oike." Some areas have no railings, so watch your step.

**2. Panoramic Views from Tojinbo Tower**
A 55-meter observation tower. The 360-degree panorama from 100 meters above sea level is spectacular. Views of the entire columnar joint formation, Sea of Japan, and on clear days, the Hakusan mountain range. Admission ¬•500.

**3. Sightseeing Boat Tour**
About 30-minute boat tour (adults ¬•1,500). The columnar joints viewed from the sea are incomparably impressive compared to land viewing. See Lion Rock, Candle Rock, and other strange rocks up close.

**4. Cross to Oshima Island**
The sacred island "Oshima" floating about 1 kilometer offshore. Cross via a 224-meter vermillion bridge to visit Ominato Shrine and other attractions. Island circumference takes about 30 minutes on foot.

### Spending Time as a Power Spot

**Deep Breathing at the Cliff's Edge**: In a safe spot, breathe in deeply the sea breeze from the Sea of Japan. Experience the sensation of body and mind being purified.

**Take Time to Gaze at the Sea**: While watching the rough waves, empty your mind. Worries become relativized and the heart feels lighter.

**Pray at Oshima**: Offer prayers quietly at Ominato Shrine. Said to offer blessings for safe voyages and matchmaking.

### Dress Code and What to Bring

- **Clothing**: Wind is often strong, so bring layers. Wear comfortable walking shoes for cliff walking. High heels are dangerous.
- **What to Bring**: Camera, hat, sunscreen (summer), warm clothing (autumn/winter)
- **Note**: Some areas have no railings. Be very careful of your footing when taking selfies or photos.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does the parking lot get crowded?**
A: Golden Week, Obon, and autumn foliage seasons are crowded. Early morning or weekdays recommended. About 500 spaces available.

**Q: Do sightseeing boats require reservations?**
A: No reservation needed, same-day boarding available. However, boats may be cancelled on high-wave days. Many closure days in winter, so please confirm.

**Q: Is it okay with children?**
A: Some cliff areas have no railings, so caution is needed with small children. Combining with Echizen Matsushima Aquarium makes for family-friendly enjoyment.

**Q: Can I enjoy it on rainy days?**
A: The shopping street and tower can be enjoyed, but cliff walking is slippery and dangerous. Sightseeing boats may also be cancelled. Sunny days are recommended.
